About Me

I am originally from Belarus, where I began my career in a completely different field — energy engineering and government energy and gas supervision.

While working as a State Energy Inspector, I realized that my passion was elsewhere. In my free time I started teaching myself programming, beginning with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, with React becoming the first framework I learned.

After completing my contract, I successfully transitioned into software engineering and joined IBA Group as a Front-End Developer, working on enterprise financial and pension automation solutions for Marsh McLennan. This period became one of the most exciting chapters of my professional life, combining software development, teamwork, travel, and international collaboration after relocating with my team to Georgia.

Winning the U.S. Diversity Visa Lottery gave me the opportunity to start a new chapter in the United States. After immigrating, I joined the New York Army National Guard to accelerate my integration into American society while continuing to pursue my long-term goal of returning to software engineering.

Alongside military service and multiple state missions supporting agencies such as DHS and DOCCS, I enrolled at Queens College to continue my Computer Science education and completed the Microsoft Software and Systems Academy (MSSA) Cloud Application Development program to strengthen my technical skills.

Today I am focused on building practical, reliable, and user-friendly software solutions while seeking opportunities in full-stack, front-end, or back-end development.

Outside of technology, I organize community choir events as a hobby, bringing together large groups of people through music and shared experiences.
